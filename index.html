COPD risk assessment

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease is a group of lung conditions that cause breathing difficulties. Many people don't realise they have it, with 1.2 million currently diagnosed in England but potentially three times as many people undiagnosed.

This is a short set of questions designed to give a broad indication as to whether you should have a chat with a doctor about your COPD risk.

Low risk

Your risk score suggests a low COPD risk. This score alone does not suggest a need to see a doctor, but if you have any concerns you should seek advice regardless.

At risk

Your risk score suggests you are at a moderately higher risk of COPD. Perhaps one in five people with your score will have COPD. We recommend you see a doctor so they can give you a more accurate assessment.

Assessment method derived from:
Haroon S, Adab P, Riley RD, et al Predicting risk of COPD in primary care: development and validation of a clinical risk score BMJ Open Respiratory Research 2015;2:e000060. doi: 10.1136/bmjresp-2014-000060
